Frequently Asked Questions

Common causes of bathroom drain clogs include hair, soap scum, toothpaste, dirt, and foreign objects. These materials can accumulate over time and restrict water flow.

To prevent clogs, regularly clean your drains, use a drain cover to catch hair, avoid pouring grease or oily substances down the drain, and periodically flush the drain with hot water.

If your bathroom drain is clogged, try using a plunger or a plumber's snake to clear the obstruction. Baking soda and vinegar can also be effective homemade solutions.

Chemical drain cleaners can be harsh and may damage pipes if used frequently. It's often better to start with mechanical methods like plunging or snaking, and use chemicals only as a last resort.

You should call a professional plumber if DIY methods fail, the clog is recurrent, you notice a bad odor, or multiple drains are slow simultaneously, indicating a larger issue.
