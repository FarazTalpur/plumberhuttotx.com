Frequently Asked Questions

You can try using a plunger, a plumber's snake, or a mixture of baking soda and vinegar to unclog a drain without using harsh chemicals.

Some common signs of a clogged drain include slow draining water, gurgling noises, foul odors coming from the drain, and water backing up in sinks or tubs.

If you have tried DIY methods and the clog persists, or if you suspect a more serious issue such as a sewer line blockage, it is best to call a professional plumber for clogged drain repair.

To prevent clogged drains, avoid putting grease, oil, hair, food scraps, and other debris down the drain. Use drain guards or screens to catch debris, and regularly flush drains with hot water to prevent buildup.

Chemical drain cleaners can be effective, but they can also be harsh on pipes and harmful to the environment. It is best to try alternative methods first before resorting to chemical drain cleaners.
