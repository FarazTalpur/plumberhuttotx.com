Understanding the Common Causes of Clogged Drains

To tackle clogged drains effectively, it's crucial first to understand their causes. Drains can become clogged due to:

  • Accumulation of Hair and Soap : Hair and soap residue often collect in bathroom sinks and showers, leading to slow drainage and blockages.

  • Grease and Food Particles : Kitchen sinks are prone to clogs due to grease, fat, and food particles sticking to the insides of pipes.

  • Foreign Objects : Items like toys, sanitary products, and wipes can accidentally make their way into drains, causing obstructions.

  • Tree Root Intrusion : Tree roots seek moisture in sewer lines, sometimes causing significant blockages and damage.

Understanding these causes can help in preventing future issues and knowing when to seek professional help.

Hydro Jetting

Hydro jetting is a powerful cleaning method that uses high-pressure water jets to dislodge stubborn blockages and clean pipe walls thoroughly. This technique is effective for:

Snaking and Augering

For clogs that are particularly stubborn or hard to reach, drain snakes and augers provide an effective solution. These flexible tools can navigate through pipes to either break up or pull out blockages.

Video Camera Inspection

To accurately diagnose and locate the source of a drain clog, we employ video camera inspections. This technology allows us to see inside pipes and determine the exact nature and location of the blockage for targeted repairs.

To prevent clogs, use drain covers, avoid pouring grease and coffee grounds down the sink, and perform regular maintenance like cleaning and using enzyme cleaners.

Call a professional if home remedies do not work, if there is a recurring issue, if multiple drains are clogged, or if you notice foul odors and unusual noises.
