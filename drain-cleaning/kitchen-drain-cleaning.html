Frequently Asked Questions

Kitchen drains often clog due to a build-up of grease, food particles, soap scum, and other debris. Over time, these materials can accumulate and obstruct the flow of water.

You can try using a mixture of baking soda and vinegar, followed by hot water, to break down the clog. Ensure to let it sit for 15-30 minutes before rinsing with hot water.

While chemical drain cleaners can be effective, they can also be harsh on your plumbing, potentially leading to damage over time. It's often safer to use natural remedies or call a professional if the clog persists.

Regular maintenance is key. Clean your kitchen drain once a week with a mild mixture of baking soda and vinegar, and perform a more thorough cleaning every few months to prevent buildup.

Avoid pouring grease, coffee grounds, and large food particles down the drain. Use a drain strainer to catch debris and run hot water down the drain after each use to help prevent clogging.
