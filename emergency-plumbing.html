Frequently Asked Questions

A plumbing emergency is typically any situation where immediate attention is required to prevent water damage or health risks, such as burst pipes, major leaks, sewer backups, or lack of running water.

To temporarily fix a leaking pipe, you can wrap it with a rubber patch or use pipe repair tape. Placing a bucket underneath to catch drips can also help minimize damage until a professional can assess the situation.

If your toilet overflows, turn off the water valve located behind the toilet to stop the water flow. Then, try to remove any blockage using a plunger. If the problem persists, contact an emergency plumber.

To prevent frozen pipes, ensure proper insulation for exposed pipes, let faucets drip during extreme cold, and keep your home adequately heated. Sealing drafts around windows and doors can also help.

While chemical drain cleaners can be a temporary solution for clearing clogs, they are often harsh and can damage your pipes. It's best to use a plunger or plumbing snake and consult a plumber for persistent issues.
