Frequently Asked Questions

As soon as you discover a burst pipe, you should first turn off the main water supply to prevent further water damage. Next, turn off electrical appliances and avoid any waterlogged areas to reduce the risk of electrical hazards. Finally, try to contain the water using buckets or towels until professional help arrives.

While homeowners can attempt temporary fixes such as using pipe repair clamps or tape to stop leaks, it’s highly recommended to call a professional plumber for a permanent repair to ensure that the job is done safely and correctly.

Pipes can burst due to several reasons including freezing temperatures causing water inside to expand, high water pressure leading to pipe stress, corrosion weakening the pipe over time, or physical damage from construction activities.

To prevent pipes from bursting, insulate pipes in unheated areas, keep indoor temperatures consistent, drain outdoor hoses and shut off exterior valves before winter, regularly inspect pipes for leaks or corrosion, and maintain a steady water pressure in your home’s plumbing system.

Signs of a burst pipe include a sudden decrease in water pressure, unusual water spots on ceilings or walls, sounds of running water when all taps are off, and unexpected increases in water bills. It’s crucial to address these signs promptly to minimize damage.
