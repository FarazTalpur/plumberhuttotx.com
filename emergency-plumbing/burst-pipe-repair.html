Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a burst pipe include water damage, low water pressure, strange noises, and high water bills.

To prevent burst pipes, insulate pipes in cold areas, keep faucets dripping during freezing temperatures, and maintain a consistent temperature in your home.

Locate the main water shut-off valve in your home and turn it clockwise to shut off the water supply. It's important to know the location of this valve ahead of time.

While minor leaks may be repairable with pipe tape or epoxy, it's recommended to contact a professional plumber for burst pipe repair to ensure proper and lasting repairs.

The cost of burst pipe repair can vary depending on the extent of the damage and the materials needed. It's best to get multiple quotes from reputable plumbers to compare prices.
