Frequently Asked Questions

To install a kitchen faucet, you typically need an adjustable wrench, basin wrench, Phillips and flathead screwdrivers, plumber's putty or silicone sealant, Teflon tape, and a bucket or towel for any water spills.

To ensure a leak-free installation, make sure all connections are secure and use plumber's putty or silicone sealant around the sink edges. It's also important to use Teflon tape on threaded pipe connections to prevent leaks.

Yes, many modern kitchen faucets come with easy-to-follow instructions, making them possible to install without professional help. However, if you encounter complex plumbing issues or are unsure, consulting a professional is recommended.

When choosing a kitchen faucet, consider the sink configuration, the size and reach of the faucet neck, water efficiency, style and finish to match your kitchen decor, and additional features such as pull-down sprayers or touchless operation.

First, turn off the water supply and disconnect the water lines. Use a basin wrench to remove the mounting nuts under the sink. Detach the faucet and clean the area before installing the new faucet. Keep a bucket or towel handy to catch any remaining water.
