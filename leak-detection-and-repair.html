Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a leak in a plumbing system include water stains on ceilings or walls, musty odors, unexplained increases in water bills, and the sound of running water when no fixtures are in use.

You can locate a leak in your plumbing system by checking for visible signs of water damage, using a leak detection device, monitoring your water meter for unexplained fluctuations, and hiring a professional leak detection service.

It is important to repair a leak promptly to prevent further water damage to your property, avoid mold growth, conserve water, and save money on water bills.

Common causes of leaks in plumbing systems include deteriorating pipe joints, corrosion, high water pressure, tree root intrusions, and poor installation or maintenance.

You can prevent leaks in your plumbing system by maintaining your pipes, avoiding putting stress on them, monitoring your water bills for unusual spikes, and scheduling regular inspections with a professional plumber.
