Frequently Asked Questions

LDAR is a program used primarily in industries to identify, locate, and repair leaks in equipment to minimize the release of fugitive emissions.

LDAR is important because it helps prevent the release of harmful emissions into the atmosphere, thereby protecting the environment and reducing health risks to humans.

Common equipment includes pumps, compressors, valves, connectors, and other components that may leak gases or vapors.

The frequency of LDAR surveys varies depending on regulatory requirements and company policies, but they are often conducted quarterly or annually.

Common methods include using portable detection instruments like infrared cameras, ultrasonic detectors, and gas analyzers to identify leaks.
