Common Causes of Faucet Leaks

Understanding why leaks occur assists in preventative measures and better maintenance strategies. Common causes include:

  • Worn Out Washers : Over time, continual friction wears down washers, causing leaks. Replacing these small components can significantly impact leak prevention.
  • Corroded Valve Seat : The valve seat connects the faucet to the spout; corrosion here can lead to leaks around the spout area.
  • Loose Parts : The continuous use of faucets can loosen components, leading to leaks. Regular maintenance helps identify and tighten these parts.
  • Faulty O-Ring : This small disc around the stem screw is another potential cause, especially prevalent in cartridge faucets.
  • Mineral Deposits : Hard water, common in certain areas of Hutto, TX, can leave mineral deposits that cause parts to corrode or block water flow.
