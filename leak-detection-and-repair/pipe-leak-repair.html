Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a pipe leak include unexplained increases in your water bill, visible mold or mildew, damp spots or water stains on walls or ceilings, and the sound of running water when no faucets are on.

For a temporary fix, you can use pipe repair tape, a pipe repair clamp, or an epoxy putty. Make sure to turn off the water supply before attempting any temporary repairs.

You should call a professional if the leak is significant, you cannot locate the source, temporary fixes are not working, or if the leak is causing damage to your property.

Pipes can leak due to corrosion, high water pressure, extreme temperature changes, ground movement, and damage from construction or accidents.

To prevent future leaks, regularly inspect your plumbing for signs of wear, ensure pipes are properly insulated, keep the water pressure at a safe level, and avoid using chemical drain cleaners.
