Essential Slab Leak Repair Services

Detection

Effective slab leak repair starts with precise detection. Advanced techniques, including electronic leak detection and acoustic sensors, pinpoint leak locations without invasive measures.

Pipe Replacement

When leaks are identified, sections of the compromised pipe may require replacement. High-quality copper or PEX piping ensures durability and reliability in repaired systems.

Rerouting

In cases where direct repair proves impractical, rerouting the plumbing line above the slab offers a long-term, cost-effective solution, minimizing disruption within the home.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a slab leak include unexplained increases in your water bill, water pooling around the foundation, damp or warm spots on the floor, low water pressure, and the sound of running water when all faucets are off.

Slab leaks can be caused by several factors such as poor installation of plumbing pipes, soil shifting, corrosion of pipes due to chemical reactions between the metal and water, and high water pressure.

Slab leak detection typically involves using specialized equipment like electronic listening devices, infrared thermography, and pressure sensors to locate the exact source of the leak without causing extensive damage to the property.

Common methods for repairing a slab leak include spot repair, re-piping or rerouting the plumbing line, and lining or coating the pipe with an epoxy-based material. The choice of method depends on the severity and location of the leak.

The cost to repair a slab leak can vary widely depending on factors such as the location and severity of the leak, the repair method chosen, and regional pricing differences. On average, repairs may range from $500 to several thousand dollars.
