Frequently Asked Questions

A slab leak is a water leak that occurs underneath the concrete foundation of a building.

Common signs of a slab leak include sudden increases in water bills, the sound of running water when no fixtures are in use, and wet or warm spots on the floor.

Slab leaks are typically repaired by either epoxy pipe coating, re-routing or repiping, or jackhammering the concrete to access and repair the damaged pipe.

The cost of slab leak repair can vary depending on the location and extent of the leak, but it typically ranges from $1,000 to $6,000.

In some cases, slab leak repair may be covered by homeowners insurance, particularly if the leak was sudden and accidental. However, coverage can vary so it's best to check with your insurance provider.
