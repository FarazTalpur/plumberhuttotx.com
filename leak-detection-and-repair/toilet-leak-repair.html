Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a toilet leak include water on the floor around the base of the toilet, a constantly running toilet, and a noticeable increase in water usage on your water bill.

Common causes of toilet leaks include a damaged wax ring, a loose toilet flange, a cracked toilet tank, or a faulty toilet fill valve.

Yes, many toilet leaks can be fixed by replacing simple parts like the wax ring, flange, tank bolts, or fill valve. However, if you are unsure or uncomfortable with the repair, it's best to hire a professional plumber.

The cost of repairing a toilet leak can vary depending on the cause of the leak and the extent of the damage. On average, it can cost anywhere from $150 to $400 to repair a toilet leak.

To prevent toilet leaks, regularly check for water pooling around the base of the toilet, avoid using harsh chemicals in the tank, and promptly repair any running toilets. Regular maintenance and inspections can help catch leaks early on.
