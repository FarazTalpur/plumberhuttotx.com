Frequently Asked Questions

The best irrigation system for your lawn depends on factors such as the size and shape of your lawn, the type of plants and grass you have, and your local climate. Consulting with a professional can help you assess these factors to determine whether a drip system, sprinkler system, or a combination is most suitable.

Regular maintenance of an irrigation system includes checking for leaks, cleaning clogged nozzles, adjusting spray patterns and times as seasons change, inspecting the water pressure, and ensuring the system controller is working properly. Winterizing your system in colder climates to prevent freezing is also crucial.

To conserve water, consider installing a smart irrigation controller that adjusts watering schedules based on local weather conditions. Use rain sensors to prevent watering when it rains, select drought-resistant plants, and regularly check your system for leaks and inefficiencies.

The frequency of watering depends on factors such as climate, soil type, and plant variety. Generally, lawns require about 1 inch of water per week, including rainfall. Watering early in the morning or late in the evening minimizes evaporation, maximizing efficiency.

Common signs include irregular water pressure, unusually high water bills, areas of the lawn that are overly dry or too wet, visible leaks or water pooling, and sprinkler heads that are not functioning or are spraying erratically. Addressing these issues promptly can prevent further damage.
