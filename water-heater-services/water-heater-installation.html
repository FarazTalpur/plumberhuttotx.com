Frequently Asked Questions

The size of the water heater depends on the number of people in the household and the peak water usage times. As a general rule, a 40-50 gallon tank is suitable for a 2-3 person household, while a 50-80 gallon tank is better for 4-5 people.

The installation of a new water heater typically takes between 2 to 4 hours, depending on the type of heater and the complexity of the installation.

Yes, most local codes require a permit to replace or install a new water heater. It's essential to check with local building codes or consult with your installer about the permit requirements.

A traditional water heater stores and preheats water in a tank, while a tankless water heater heats water on demand without storing it. Tankless heaters are typically more energy-efficient but may have higher upfront costs.

Most water heaters have a lifespan of about 8-12 years. However, this can vary based on factors like maintenance, installation quality, and water quality. It’s best to monitor performance and consult a professional to determine when a replacement is needed.
